Traveling to Japan

In spring 2024, I had the incredible opportunity to study abroad at Sophia University in Tokyo — and it was truly one of the most unforgettable chapters of my life. Living in Japan for a semester wasn’t just about taking classes; it was about stepping into a culture I’d admired for years, navigating the Tokyo train system like a pro (eventually), and finding a second home halfway across the world. From cherry blossoms in Yoyogi Park to late-night ramen runs in Shinjuku, every day was a new adventure.

What made the experience even more meaningful was being able to apply the Japanese I’d studied before the trip — shoutout to Genki, WaniKani, and Anki for laying the groundwork! Speaking the language, even just a little, opened up so many doors and helped me connect more deeply with the people and places around me. Whether it was chatting with classmates, ordering food with confidence, or getting lost (and un-lost) with the help of kind locals, I grew so much — both linguistically and personally. If you’re even thinking about studying abroad in Japan, I can’t recommend it enough.
